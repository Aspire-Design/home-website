F.A.Q.

  • Why should I invest in a website?

    A website is a valuable investment that can continue to reinforce your current marketing strategies for a very little cost. You should expect a 12-month return on your website investment through cost savings alone.

    The Internet is now the place people turn to when looking for information on specific products or services in order to make a purchase decision. If your company or association isn't on-line or if your present website is not professional looking or isn't easily found by the major search engines, you are going to lose sales. A professional website has a strong value proposition to any organization. It will help you reach and retain customers.

  • Why should I use a professional website design company to build my website when I know someone who could do it for cheap (maybe even free)?

    Would you trust a part-time electrician to wire your new house? We aim for longterm success and growth. We want to build a brand that will stand strong for the future and won't need a re-branding in a few years. We spend our time efficiently enabling us to put more effort towards building the strongest image rather than figuring out what the quickest way to make a passable website is.

  • I am totally new to this "website thing". How does the whole process work?

    Glad you asked, check out what our process looks like.

  • My customers hardly use the internet, what would I gain from getting one now?

    Demographics are changing. There is an entire generation of consumers whose first instinct is to visit the Internet to make a purchasing decision. At the very least, a website is like a virtual business card that provides detailed information about your business. If your competition is online and you do not have a website, you will lose sales.

  • Will I own my website?

    Yes! We absolutely want to stand behind you for the life of your website however, if you decide you would like to take your website to another hosting provider we will happily assist you in making that transition.
